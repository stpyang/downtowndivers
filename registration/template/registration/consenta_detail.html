{% if consent.consent_compressed_gas_risk %}{{ consent.member.initials }}{% endif %}

I understand and acknowledge that breathing industrial compressed air and/or Compressed Breathing Gases can be lethal due to a number of factors, including but not limited to, possible impurities in it or the manner in which such air or gases are filled in Diving Cylinders. Specifically I understand and am completely advised of the following:

  • special precautions need to be taken with gases other than air;

  • oxygen in high concentrations is a major cause of fire and rust;

  • oxygen should be very carefully stored in cylinders that are certified and labeled for oxygen use;

  • oxygen and other gases must be carefully transferred from one cylinder to another;

  • gas mixtures containing proportions of oxygen other than 21% could be extremely dangerous to divers;

  • all cylinders must be labeled with their composition after filling;

  • contaminated air or gases (eg., carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, oil and lubricants from the compressor) at depth can be fatal;

  • the blast caused by a sudden release of the gas pressure inside a diving cylinder makes them very dangerous;

  • the greatest risk of explosion exists at the time the cylinders are being filled;

  • keeping the cylinder slightly pressurized at all times reduces the possibility of contaminating the inside of the cylinder with corrosive agents, such as salt water, or toxic material, including but not limited to other substances, such as oils, poisonous gases, fungi or bacteria;

  • use of Diving Cylinders containing improperly blended or incorrect proportions of Compressed Breathing Gases may be considered an ultrahazardous activity and could result in serious injury or death.
